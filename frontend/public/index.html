THESIS

Why open-source derivatives infrastructure matters.

The derivatives market runs on proprietary systems that nobody can audit. That's a problem for markets, regulators, and participants alike.

01
The model is the moat.
Regulators are deeply suspicious of proprietary black boxes. An open-source platform where every pricing formula is auditable, reproducible, and peer-reviewed creates trust that no proprietary system can match.
02
Blockchain compresses XVA.
MPoR compression from 10 days to sub-day eliminates five of the six regulatory justifications for the margin period. CVA drops ~70%. FVA drops ~90%. The same trade with a blockchain-confirmed counterparty is worth materially more.
03
Democratise institutional infrastructure.
Smaller banks, hedge funds, university finance departments, and emerging market participants deserve the same pricing infrastructure as tier-1 dealers. No licence fees. No vendor lock-in. No black boxes.
04
Built by practitioners.
15+ years across XVA, CCR, ISDA SIMM, PNL attribution, and market risk. Every model choice reflects production experience at institutional level. Not a research project — a production platform.
